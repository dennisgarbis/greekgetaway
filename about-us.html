FAQ

Mainland/Northern Greece

2

Peloponnese

3

Best Way to Get Around

1

Car

Might be the most dangerous, as Greek drivers are impatient, hardly following the rules of the road. Steep roads, blind intersections, tight streets, and heavy traffic make driving a car a difficult task for most.

2

Bus

Many busses leave the terminals in Athens traveling across the country. Though slow, and sometimes scary, the price isn't too expensive and leaves the headache of getting around to the professionals.

3

Plane

Reaching some of the furthest islands takes a long while with the boat. Luckily planes can get you anywhere you want to be within an hour. Most of the larger, more popular islands have airports, allowing flight travel to be convenient.

4

Train

Outside of Athens, train travel has not caught on like the rest or Europe. Good luck.

5

Ferry Boat

Take your car onto the ferry as you travel from the mainland to your favorite island. Just be careful parking as they like to get you as close as possible to the other cars.

6

Donkey

Cheap, yet noisy, a donkey is best used to help carry your stuff around as you hike up the mountains.

Important Stuff To Know

1

Is Greece in the EU and what currency do they use?

Greece has been in the EU since 1981 and have been using the Euro since 2002.

2

Do I need a Visa to Enter Greece?

If you're an EU citizen or a citizen of a country that has visa-free travel, then no.

3

What is the Best Way to Travel Between Islands?

Ferries are the best, as you can take your car with, are the cheapest, but also not the quickest. Some of the more popular island have airports, and save you time, but also are more expensive.

4

When is the Best Time to Visit Greece?

The best time is late spring to early summer. The weather isn't as hot and you're avoiding peak tourist season, which brings a lot of crowds and higher prices.

5

How Long Should I Spend in Greece?

You need at least a week to see the sites in Athens and visit a nearby island. The best would be at least two weeks, giving you time to visit a few islands, and either northern or southern Greece. Ideally, spending a month is best, as you won't be in a rush and can enjoy all that Greece has to offer.
