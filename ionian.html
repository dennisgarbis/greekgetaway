IONIAN
ISLANDS

Kefalonia, Zakynthos, Lefkada, Ithaca, Corfu

Consisting of seven islands, the Ionian region is located between Italy and Greece. It has overcome many different rulers, from the Romans, Venetians, British, and Germans, and back to the Greeks. Each island has its own unique features, and are some of the largest in Greece.

Kefalonia

Breathtaking scenery, lush forests, and high cliffs adorn the largest Ionian Island of Kefalonia. Many ports connect Kefalonia to the mainland and other islands. The world famous Myrtos Beach is here, as well as many others. Steep mountains, small villages, and some of the best views are found here.

Things to See

  • Myrtos Beach
  • Melissani Cave
  • St. Gerasimos
  • Mt. Ainos National Park
  • Makris Gialos Beach
  • Antisamos Beach
  • Argostoli
  • Lixouri
  • Fiskardo
  • Assos

Zakynthos

Located just south of Kefalonia is Zakynthos. Steep mountains are found on one side while its most famous beach is found on the other. St. Dionysios, my saint's name, is also from this island.

Things to See

  • Navagio Beach
  • Kalamaki Beach
  • Blue Caves
  • Marathonisi
  • St. Dionysios
  • Banana Beach

Lefkada

Although it is technically an island, Lefkada is connected to the mainland by a small causeway and a bridge. Plenty of beaches, and of course steep cliffs like the other islands, can be found here.

Things to See

  • Porto Katsiki
  • Egremni Beach
  • Monastery of Panagia
  • Nydri Waterfalls
  • Milos Beach
  • Mikros Gialos
  • Kathisma Beach

Ithaca

Ithaka is home to Homer's Odysseus. Crystal clear water, beaches only accessible by sea, and tranquil towns offer a relaxing vacation.

Things to See

  • Filiatro Beach
  • Kathara Monastery
  • Gedaki Beach
  • Homer's School

Corfu

Also known as Kerkira, Corfu is the northernmost of the Ionian Islands. Many foreign powers have had control of the island, including the Venetians, French, and British.

Things to See/Do

  • Corfu Old Town
  • St. Spyridon
  • Archilleion
  • Mount Pantokrator
  • Paleokastritsa Monastery
